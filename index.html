About This Website ▾

Welcome to my portfolio! I built this space to be a clear, living snapshot of my work and how I think. A site like this carries more context than a PDF resume: it shows personality, provides room for detail, and evolves with new experience. It’s also a place to share projects and the tools I use every day. Under the hood it’s simple: HTML and CSS with a touch of JavaScript for tasteful animations and accessibility, hosted on GitHub Pages. The goal is clean, fast, and easy to maintain, so the content does the talking.

About My Professional Career ▾

In a sentence: I’m a technology enthusiast, problem solver, and people-first IT professional with over six years of hands-on experience focused on IT operations, infrastructure, and process improvement. My passion for technology started back in high school. My dad ran a small PC repair business fixing everything from hardware failures to data recovery and software troubleshooting. But what left the biggest impression wasn’t just what he knew, it was how persistent he was. He could spend hours or days chasing down a problem, and the moment he found the fix? Pure joy. That passion for discovering solutions inspired my own path: a career where I could solve real problems, support others, and keep systems running efficiently, both operationally and systematically. I earned two Associate degrees from Volunteer State Community College and hold multiple IT certifications. As college came to a close, I landed my first internship as an IT Field Engineer. Since then, I’ve gained experience in a wide range of environments, from a corporate IT Service Desk to two startups in infrastructure and engineering. Today, I serve as a Senior IT Systems Administrator at one of the world’s leading cybersecurity companies.

About My Personal Life ▾

Life isn’t just work; I have a few side quests too! I’m a big fan of sports, gaming, and travel. I love baseball, basketball, and soccer. I generally support South Florida sports teams, and while living in Nashville I (try) to support our local teams too. One of my long-term goals is to visit all 30 Major League Baseball stadiums. So far, I’ve hit 15… which means I’ve still got a lot of miles (and hot dogs) to go. When it comes to gaming, I was once a hardcore PC gamer. These days I stick to consoles. Sure, I miss the buttery-smooth frame rates, but I don’t miss the weekly and sometimes daily firmware, driver, and software updates. I want to boot up and play, not debug and wait. Whether you’re here to talk tech or trade ballpark rankings, I’m just as happy to connect personally as I am professionally. You’ll find links to my social profiles right on the homepage!
